<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold text-foreground mt-10 mb-3">Definition: What is a DOT annual inspection actually checking?</h2>
<p class="text-base leading-relaxed"><strong>DOT annual inspection definition:</strong> A federally mandated annual inspection of all commercial motor vehicles (CMVs) covering every safety-critical system per FMCSA standards. Required by law for every truck operating in interstate commerce. The inspector verifies that brakes, steering, suspension, lighting, tires, wheels, frame, fuel system, exhaust, coupling devices, and all related components meet federal minimums.</p>
<p class="text-base leading-relaxed">The annual inspection happens at a qualified shop on a scheduled day. A <em>roadside</em> inspection happens whenever law enforcement or DOT pulls a truck — at weigh stations, during traffic stops, or at random checkpoints. Both inspections look at the same equipment, but the roadside version is unscheduled and the consequences for failure are immediate: out-of-service tag, no further movement until corrected. According to <a href="https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/regulations/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="underline" style="color: #991B1B;">FMCSA regulations</a>, an annual inspection certificate must be on file and the truck must display the most recent decal.</p>

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold text-foreground mt-10 mb-3">Common mistakes: Why Phoenix fleets still fail inspections</h2>
<p class="text-base leading-relaxed">Even fleets that have "a PM program" still rack up DOT violations. The recurring failure modes:</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 text-base leading-relaxed">
  <li><strong>Treating PM as oil change + tire rotation only.</strong> Real PM includes brake measurement, lighting check, fluid analysis, coolant chemistry, suspension component check. If your shop doesn't document each system, they're not doing it.</li>
  <li><strong>Skipping the manual brake stroke measurement.</strong> Automatic slack adjusters fail silently. Pushrod stroke must be measured by hand at the 50K-mile PM and annual inspection. Visual-only check misses it.</li>
  <li><strong>Ignoring lighting harness wear.</strong> Phoenix UV + heat degrades wire insulation faster than rated. Connectors corrode. Marker lights flicker. The truck passes a quick visual but fails a real inspection that operates each circuit.</li>
  <li><strong>Forgetting driver qualification paperwork.</strong> DOT inspectors check paperwork as carefully as the truck. Expired medical card = OOS even if the truck is perfect. Annual MVR review missing = compliance violation.</li>
  <li><strong>Not maintaining digital inspection records.</strong> If your shop can't produce 12 months of inspection records in under 5 minutes, you have a CSA audit risk. Paper files don't survive an audit; digital records do.</li>
</ul>

<p class="text-base leading-relaxed">Driver safety inspections at roadside include cab climate functionality during high-heat months. A blown <a href="#" class="font-bold underline" style="color: #DC2626;">semi truck AC</a> isn't just uncomfortable — it's an HOS violation waiting to happen and an inspector trigger. Pre-summer AC inspection rolled into the annual PM avoids the August scramble.</p>
