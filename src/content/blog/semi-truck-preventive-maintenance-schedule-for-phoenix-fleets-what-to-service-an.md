# Semi Truck Preventive Maintenance Schedule for Phoenix Fleets: What to Service and When

A breakdown on I-10 at noon in July costs more than 6 months of PM. Here's the exact maintenance schedule Phoenix fleets use to keep trucks rolling and out of the shop — organized by interval so your drivers and shop coordinators can actually use it.

---

## Why Arizona Heat Changes the PM Math

Most OEM maintenance intervals are written for average conditions. Phoenix is not average. Ambient temps above 110°F push coolant systems, A/C compressors, and engine oil to the edge of their design range on every summer run. Turbo bearings cook faster. Belt compounds harden and crack in half the time. Tires build pressure faster during highway loads on superheated pavement.

Your **fleet PM schedule for semi trucks in Arizona** needs tighter intervals than what the factory manual shows, especially for cooling, A/C, and rubber components. Build that into your schedule before summer, not after your first July meltdown on the I-17.

---

## The Core Truck Maintenance Intervals Every Commercial Vehicle Needs

Here's how to break it down by interval. These are the minimums — adjust tighter for high-mileage units, older trucks, or severe duty cycles.

### Every Trip — Pre-Trip Inspection (15 Minutes)

Drivers should be doing this before every departure. It's federal law under [FMCSA regulations](https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/regulations/), and it's also your first line of defense against a roadside writeup.

- **Tires:** Check for visible damage, valve stems, and pressure — especially in summer when pavement temps run 150°F-plus
- **Lights:** All marker lights, headlights, brake lights, and turn signals
- **Brakes:** Listen for air leaks at pushrod seals; check brake lines for visible wear
- **Fluid levels:** Oil, coolant, DEF, washer fluid
- **Mirrors and glass:** Any cracks that affect sightlines
- **Coupling devices:** Fifth wheel lock, kingpin, glad hands

A failed pre-trip is the cheapest breakdown prevention tool you have.

---

### Every 10,000–15,000 Miles

This is your basic oil service window, though some fleets running CJ-4 oil and high-quality filters push to 20,000 miles. Know what your engine OEM recommends and don't exceed it in Arizona heat.

- **Engine oil and filter change** — Cummins, Detroit, and Paccar all have specific recommendations based on oil analysis; sample if you're extending intervals
- **Fuel filter primary and secondary** — Water in diesel is a real problem in Arizona due to temperature cycling overnight; don't skip secondary filter changes
- **Drain air tanks** — Manually verify the auto-drain is working; moisture accumulation accelerates in humid monsoon months
- **Grease chassis:** Fifth wheel, slack adjusters, kingpins, driveshaft u-joints, and steering components — every 10,000 miles or more frequently if running off-pavement

---

### Every 25,000–30,000 Miles

- **Coolant system inspection:** Check hoses for softness, swelling, or hardening. Check the pressure cap. Test freeze point and nitrite levels with test strips — Arizona trucks still need proper coolant chemistry for cavitation protection
- **Belt inspection:** Serpentine and accessory drive belts crack fast in desert heat; look for glazing and edge fraying
- **Air filter:** Check restriction indicator; desert driving loads filters faster than normal
- **A/C system pressure check:** Before summer starts, verify refrigerant charge and check the compressor clutch for slippage. If you're seeing weak cab cooling in spring, it won't get better in August. - **Brake lining measurement:** Front and rear — record it so you have trending data

---

### Every 50,000 Miles

- **Transmission fluid and filter:** Check the manufacturer spec — some automated transmissions run to 100,000 miles but verify with fluid analysis
- **Differential and steer axle fluid check:** Look for water intrusion and contamination
- **Tire rotation and alignment check:** Irregular wear on Arizona highways usually points to alignment or suspension issues, not just the tires. [Semi truck tire and brake repair services](/semi-truck-tire-and-brake-repair-services-phoenix-az) including alignment checks are part of a complete PM program
- **Brake adjustment verification:** Automatic slack adjusters fail without warning; verify stroke manually at the 50K mark regardless
- **EGR cooler and DPF inspection:** Especially on post-2010 emissions trucks. A clogged DPF means regen cycles, power loss, and eventually a $3,000 replacement if ignored

---

### Every 100,000 Miles or Annually

- **DOT annual inspection:** Required by law. At CT Shop we run full [DOT inspections in Phoenix, AZ](/dot-inspections-phoenix-az) covering brakes, lighting, tires, steering, suspension, coupling, and all federally regulated systems. Make sure your paperwork is clean and your driver qualification files are up to date
- **Injector performance check:** Carbon buildup on injectors shows up as rough idle, black smoke, or poor fuel economy — get a diagnostic read before they fail on the road
- **Coolant flush:** Full drain and refill with correct extended-life coolant per OEM spec
- **Turbocharger inspection:** Check for shaft play and oil seals — turbos on high-hour desert engines see significant heat cycling
- **Suspension bushings and mounts:** Worn rubber mounts show up as cab vibration and accelerated tire wear
